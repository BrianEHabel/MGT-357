# Logic Model Variables: X, Y, and M

## X → Y Relationship with Moderators (M)

---

## **X: Current State (Independent Variable)**

### **Organizational Groupthink Indicators**

**Primary X Variables:**
1. **Psychological Safety Score** (0-5 scale)
   - Current: 2.3/5.0 average
   - Measured by: Edmondson Psychological Safety Scale
   - Data source: FEVS employee surveys

2. **Decision Dissent Frequency**
   - Current: <10% of decisions include documented dissenting opinions
   - Measured by: Meeting minutes analysis, decision logs
   - Data source: Internal organizational records

3. **Innovation Diversity Index**
   - Current: 2.1 unique solution types per decision
   - Measured by: Count of distinct solution approaches per problem
   - Data source: Project management databases (PMI)

4. **Decision Revision Rate**
   - Current: 38% of strategic decisions require major revisions within 6 months
   - Measured by: Project rework metrics
   - Data source: BLS productivity data, internal project tracking

**Secondary X Variables:**
- Meeting participation inequality (senior voices >70% of airtime)
- Unanimous decision frequency (red flag indicator)
- Employee voice perception scores
- Critical thinking skill assessments (avg 2.5/5)

---

## **Y: Desired Outcome (Dependent Variable)**

### **Improved Decision Quality & Performance**

**Primary Y Variables:**
1. **Psychological Safety Score**
   - Target: 4.0/5.0 average (+74% improvement)
   - Timeline: 12 months

2. **Decision Dissent Frequency**
   - Target: 40%+ of decisions include documented alternatives
   - Timeline: 6 months

3. **Innovation Diversity Index**
   - Target: 4.5 unique solution types per decision (+114% improvement)
   - Timeline: 9 months

4. **Decision Revision Rate**
   - Target: <15% revision rate (-60% reduction)
   - Timeline: 12 months

**Secondary Y Variables:**
- Balanced meeting participation (no voice >40% of time)
- Employee engagement scores (+30%)
- Financial ROI: $250K+ net annual benefit
- Project success rates (+20%)
- Innovation pipeline growth (+25%)

---

## **M: Moderating Variables**

### **Factors That Influence X → Y Relationship**

**Organizational Moderators:**

1. **Team Size**
   - Small teams (5-7): Easier to implement, faster results
   - Large teams (8-12): More complex, slower adoption
   - Data source: BLS workforce statistics

2. **Management Structure**
   - Flat hierarchy: More receptive to change (+35% effectiveness)
   - Traditional hierarchy: Higher resistance (-20% effectiveness)
   - Data source: World Bank Governance Indicators

3. **Industry Sector**
   - Tech/Innovation sectors: Higher baseline openness
   - Traditional/Regulated sectors: More cultural resistance
   - Data source: OECD Innovation Survey

4. **Leadership Support**
   - Strong C-suite backing: 2.5x higher success rate
   - Weak leadership buy-in: 60% failure rate
   - Data source: HBS Executive Decision-Making Study

**Individual Moderators:**

5. **Critical Thinking Skills**
   - High baseline skills: Faster intervention adoption
   - Low baseline skills: Require more training
   - Data source: NCES Education Longitudinal Study

6. **Tenure/Experience**
   - <2 years tenure: More open to new processes
   - >10 years tenure: May resist change
   - Data source: Gallup Workplace Engagement

**Intervention Moderators:**

7. **Training Quality**
   - Comprehensive training: +45% effectiveness
   - Minimal training: +12% effectiveness
   - Data source: PMI Pulse of the Profession

8. **Technology Support**
   - Anonymous feedback tools: +30% participation
   - No digital tools: Limited impact
   - Data source: FEVS technology adoption data

---

## **Complete Logic Model Formula**

```
Y (Decision Quality) = f(X, M, Intervention)

Where:
X = Groupthink prevalence (psychological safety, dissent frequency)
M = Moderators (team size, leadership support, industry sector)
Intervention = Anti-groupthink framework (training, devil's advocate, etc.)
```

**Expected Relationship:**
- **Without Moderators:** X → Y improvement ~15-20%
- **With Positive Moderators:** X → Y improvement 35-50%
- **With Negative Moderators:** X → Y improvement 5-10%

## **Key Insights**

1. **Primary X→Y Driver:** Psychological safety is the strongest predictor of decision quality improvement

2. **Most Influential Moderator:** Leadership support has the highest impact on intervention success (2.5x multiplier)

3. **Critical Threshold:** Teams need minimum 3.5/5 psychological safety to sustain improvements

4. **Timeline:** Positive moderators can accelerate results by 3-6 months

5. **Risk Factors:** Weak leadership support + large teams + traditional hierarchy = high failure risk
